• 1878 - Club founded by members of the Carriage and Wagon department of the Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway (LYR) depot at Newton Heath
  • 1908 - Manchester United win their first league title
  • 1909 - United wins FA Cup final against Bristol City
  • 1910 - Old Trafford was named as the home of Manchester United
  • 1912 - Club wins their first ever Charity Shield
  • 1922 - Manchester United relegated to the Second Division
  • 1945 - Club appointed Matt Busby who demanded an unprecedented level of control over team selection, player transfers and training sessions
  • 1952 - Club wins the First division, after 41 years
  • 1968 - Manchester United became the first English (and second British) club to win the European Cup, beating Benfica in the final
  • 1986 - Alex Ferguson appointed as the manager of the club replacing Ron Atkinson
  • 1990 - Ferguson wins his first silverware in FA Cup
  • 1991 - United claimed its first Cup Winners' Cup title and competed UEFA Super Cup, beating European Cup holders Red Star Belgrade in the final
  • 1998 - Manchester United became the first team to win the Premier League, FA Cup and UEFA Champions League – "The Treble" – in the same season
  • 2008 - Club completed the European double with a 6-5 penalty shootout victory over Chelsea
  • 2013 - Club wins it's 20th league title and Ferguson retires as manager at the end of the football season
  • 2017 - Club wins the Europa League for the first time
